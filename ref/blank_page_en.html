About: welcome
In the old town centre of Tolmin, set up in a part od the former Coronini mansion, HOTEL KRN is expecting you. It was named after the mountain rising 2233m above the left bank of the river Soča, one of the most beautiful rivers in Europe. The sight of the stone wreath of the Tolmin-Bohinj mountains, the embrace of the clear waters of the Tolminka and Soča rivers which come together at Sotočje near Tolmin, the richness of the green slopes which surround the Tolmin basin, all this is creating a wonderful environment on the Southern edge of the Triglav national park and is inviting you to experience it.

In an arched castle celar from 17th century you can experience the atmosphere of the times of castles with an excellent choice of wine or home-brewed beer, made according to ancient recipes.

Must see: around Tolmin

Tolmin is the largest settlement in the Zgornje Posočje region and has been the natural centre of the Tolminska region for centuries. A walk through the town takes you through history, into the time of the patriarchs af Aquileila, the Tolmin counts and peasant rebels, the maelstrom of the 1st World War ad the soška fronta frontline.

Must see: green embrace

The unspoiled nature of the Tolminska region hides many interesting artefacts of extreme natural beauty in its green shelter. Gorges, waterfalls, springs, ravines, streams and rivers, the richness of the forests, the diversity of the geomorphologic phenomena, the cultural landscape and other witnesses to the natural processes in the past and present call to discovery and exploration. No one can resist the river Soča and its unique emerald colour: it brings guests from all over the world into the arms of the Posočje region mountains and plains.

We should preserve the characteristics of this landscape with appropriate behaviour and a respectful attitude towards our natural heritage!

Must see: walk of peace

The legacy of the soška fronta frontline with six outdoor museums, Kobarid museum and Tolmin museum, the cultural and natural heritage is connected by the Walk of peace, a well marked trail equipped with information boards, interesting for hikers and cyclists. It is dedicated to the memory of the lost soldiers of many nationalities who fought, suffered and died in this region during the 1st World War. Numerous military structures, churches and chapels, cemeteries, monuments and memorial plaques with chiselled inscriptions and images tell stories full op pain and hope.

  
 

Sightseeing hints:

Outdoor museums: Mrzli vrh, Kolovrat, Mengore

1st World War memorial sites: The Church of the Holy Spirit in Javorca, The Austro-Hungarian military cemetery at Loče, The German ossuary near Tolmin, The military chapel in Gabrje, ...

Private collections: The Tolmin bridgehead small museum, Most na Soči
 
Activities: cycling

You can never run out of ideas for cycling around the diverse landscape of one of the most beautiful parts of Slovenia, the Soča river valley. Less demanding cyclists and families can cycle along less difficult roads betwen villages, the cart tracks along the Soča river and itd affluents. More experienced riders can enjoy a wide range of mountain climbs and descents of varying difficulty. Some of them are among the most attractive in Europe.

  
  Cycling trips can be combined with the visiting of natural and cultural monuments along the trails. Some theme trails can also be experienced on a bicycle rather than on foot.
 
Activities: water & air

The entire Soča river valley is an excellent place for enjoying various sports between the emerald reflections of the river and the blue sky. For lovers of exciting experiences on untamed waters, adrenaline filled descents on the rapids and through gorges, jumps into deep pools this is true sports Eldorado. For beginners also. It offers rafting, canyoning, hydrospeed, kayak, canoe and mini raft runs, kayak school and courses. The adrenaline park can test your abilities on land.

  
 

The whiteness of the gravel beaches attracts numerous swimmers longing for refreshment in the hot summer days. During the holidays they often organise picnics on the river banks.

The wide skies spanning over the charming landscape are a paradise for paragliders and hang-gliders. Both take off points, Kobala above Tolmin and the one at Stol near Kobarid are easily accessible. They provide paragliding enthusiasts with excellent starting points for long flights above the Julian Alps and Italy. Tandem flights can offer inexperienced paragliders a genuine flying experience and panoramic views. The Kobala take off point is a popular site for national, European and World cup competitions.
 
Activities: hiking

The hills and mountains rising above the Soča river valley are full of paths and trails. Tolmin is an excellent starting point for various hikes to mountain destinations. Well marked mountain trails, paths to high mountain pastures and tourist points, theme and forest learning trails invite the visitor to surrender to the nature's pulse and wind's caress, listen to the birds singing and leaves rustling and gaze at the horizon. Some are rushing to their mountain destination with a cheerful greeting, some are fuelled by the loneliness and silence of the mountains, some enjoy the early morning in the mountains and the sight of shining rocky peaks.

Activities: recreation

Tolmin offers many possibilities for recreation in its vicinity. The marked recreation paths around Castle hill and along the river Soča run through forests in the shades of the trees and they offer the best experience in the morning hours and hours before noon, when the air is still fresh. The trails and cart tracks along the Soča and Tolminka rivers as well as numerous other walkways around Tolmin can be used for running.

Tolmin also has two sports parks and a modern multi-purpose sports hall. The Brajda sports park has an athletics and football stadium, outdoor basketball courts, handball and beach volley courts as well as two tennis courts. The Na Logu sports park has a football pitch, a smaller football pitch with artificial grass, a boules court and a racetrack with stands. The Sports hall can be used for various sport and recreational programmes. The hall can be used to train basketball, volleyball, handball and futsal. The small hall is intended for martial arts and sport dancing. There is also a fitness centre in the complex.

  
 

The quality of the sports facilities in Tolmin is proved by successful preparations of numerous sports teams and national selections, especially footballers.

The Mountaineering teaching centre is intended for young climbers and alpinists, mountaineering and tourist guides and mountain rescue team members. Throughout the year they organize sport climbing training on an artificial wall as well as sport climbing competitions.
 
Activities: white winter

When a blanket of snow softens the mountain peaks, comes the time of winter fun. The shining white slopes of the nearby ski resorts attract skiers, snowboarders, cross-country skiing and sledding enthusiasts.

At the Kanin ski centre above Bovec the skiing area are above 2000m above sea level. The skiing season lasts from December to the beginning of May. From the ski slope bathed in sunshine we can see deep into the Alps and Mediterranean sea. Apline touring and adrenaline enthusiasts can also find something for themselves here.

  
 

There is also the posibility of skiing in three countries on the Sella Nevea and Traviso ski slopes in Italy and in Arnoldstein in Austria. Skiing betwen Slovenia and Italy is possible with a single skiing ticket. The Kanin-Sella Nevea connected ski slopes are the only cross-border ski resorts in the region.

The Cerkno ski centre is a moredn family ski resort. 70 hectares of skiing areas are suitable for beginners as well as more demanding skiers. For cross-country skiing lovers there are 5kms of tracks available.
 
Activities: neighbours

In search of new experiences you can head out from Tolmin as a starting point to shorter or longer trips around the Posočje region: to the Kobaričko or Bovško regions to the North, into the Trenta valley or to Lod pod Mangartom; towards the South to Kanal, Goriška brda, Nova Gorica, Vipava, Ajdovščina and the Karst; towards the East to Cerkno and Idrija. And why not to the West beyond the former border into Benečija, Rezija or the Kanalska dolina valley?

  
 

You can treat yourself to a trip to the Gorenjska region: across the Vršič pass to Kranjska Gora, across Sorica to Bohinj and Bled. You can also take the motorail. The ride along famous Bohinj route through numerous tunnels and accross viaducts is especially picturesque. You can also take the museum train to Bled.

Local transport providers can take you to Trieste, Venice, Padova, Traviso, Klagenfurt and Villach. Or further; the choice is yours.
 
Tradition: living water

Clear water springs that never dry up keep old stories and legends. They whisper the memories of mowers and rakers who were using the living water in the meadows, the shepherds who were giving the water to their livestock on high mountain pastures, the travellers which were using the springs to freshen up, quenche their thirst and regain their strenght. The various springs were well known by our ancestors. They were living in harmony with nature and knew which water was good and nutritious, beneficial to one's well-being, good for washes and cuts and which water was appreciated and protected and the living water legacy carried on through the generations.

Tradition: high mountain pastures

High mountain pastures under the mighty mountains above the river Soča have for a thousand years been preserving the high mountain pasture tradition far away from the settlements in the valley. Here the shepherds carry the tradition of turning milk into cheese, cottage chees and butter from generation to generation. The architectural heritage of shepherd settlements, main shepherd buildings and stables is also of enormous ethnological importance.

In the historical ethnological exhibition at the Planika dairy in Kobarid the legacy on high mountain pastures and the tradition of cheese making in the Posočje region is presented.

In the summer we can taste and buy the mountain cheese, 'abriblni' (cheese clipings) and cottage cheese at the high mountain pastures. The traditional cheese-making methods can be observed with guides.

  
 

You can buy cheese, cottage cheese and whey at the village dairies in Zatolmin and at the market in Tolmin on Saturdays. Here and at various homesteads in the vicinity you can also buy products related to beekeeping, home made spirits, herb mixtures, fruit and vegetables produced in accordance with organic and boidynamic production.
